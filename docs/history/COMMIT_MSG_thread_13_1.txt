Thread 13.1 — VET frontier patch: TLSNotary Proxy mode + SCITT registration + Chathurangi-2026 references

WHAT
----
Three frontier-as-of-May-18-2026 capabilities added on top of Thread 13:

1. TLSNotary Proxy mode (alpha.15, May 10, 2026) as a 6th
   WebProofMode. Verifier-acts-as-proxy trust model. 1-2 second
   attestation (vs 3-15s for MPC mode) with weaker but explicit
   trust assumption. Pair with MPC in a k-of-n committee for
   Byzantine-tolerant trust-speed coverage.

2. First AI-governance vendor to ship per-decision SCITT
   registration. Every PERMIT/ABSTAIN/FORBID decision and every
   Agent Identity Document can be registered as a COSE_Sign1
   Signed Statement to a Transparency Service, receiving a COSE
   Receipt with RFC-9162 SHA-256 Merkle inclusion proof. Combined
   with Thread 1's hash chain and Thread 12's TEE JWT this gives
   auditors THREE INDEPENDENT VERIFICATION AXES on a single
   decision — none requiring trust in Tex.

3. ARP (Attestation Reconciliation Protocol) primitive per
   draft-hillier-scitt-arp-00 (May 2026). Cross-sovereign claim
   reconciliation across EU AI Act / NIST AI RMF / UK AISI
   registries via controlled projection functions, without raw
   register records crossing jurisdictional boundaries.

Plus honest docstring-level corrections referencing the genuine
native-PQ swap target for the credential primitive:
  Chathurangi, "Post-Quantum Traceable Anonymous Credentials from
  Lattices," IACR CIC, Jan 8 2026, DOI 10.62056/ak5wl8n4e.
