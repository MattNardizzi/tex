Thread 3 — Causal attribution as cryptographically signed post-incident evidence
================================================================================

Wires src/tex/causal into a new read-only endpoint:

    POST /v1/incidents/{decision_id}/attribute

Produces a SCITT-shaped COSE_Sign1 Signed Statement carrying a ranked
multi-candidate attribution result with LSH-Shapley blame distribution
and ARM causality-laundering detection, optionally extended with a
PTV-shaped Groth16 envelope and an NVIDIA NRAS EAT JWT attestation
binding. Hash-chained into the same evidence ledger as decisions and
contract violations via a new EvidenceRecorder.record_attribution
method with parent_evidence_hash linkage.

The /v1/guardrail hot path is unchanged. The attribution endpoint
reads the stored Decision, runs compute_attribution, signs the claim
set, and writes one new evidence row.

WHY: 2026 AI-governance vendors (Microsoft AGT v3.5.0 May 8, Zenity,
Noma, F5/CalypsoAI, CrowdStrike/Pangea, Palo Alto/Protect AI) ship
incident correlation and logging. None ships causal failure
attribution as a first-class signed audit artifact. The four pieces
this thread combines exist in research and standards drafts as of
May 18, 2026 but have not been assembled in any published
implementation or shipping product.
