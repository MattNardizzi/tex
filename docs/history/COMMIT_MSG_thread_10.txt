Thread 10 — frontier post-quantum cryptography wave

Closes the post-quantum cryptography layer beyond every shipping
competitor as of May 18, 2026. Adds the production-ready half of the
NIST PQ standards Tex left stubbed in Thread 4, plus two frontier
constructions (threshold ML-DSA, composite ML-DSA) that no AI
governance product currently implements.

Modules added
-------------
* tex.pqcrypto.ml_kem
    Full FIPS 203 binding via liboqs 0.15. ML-KEM-512/768/1024 with
    formally-verified PQCP mlkem-native backend (CBMC + HOL-Light).
    Fail-closed length validation, telemetry on every operation,
    documented implicit-rejection contract per FIPS 203 §7.3.

* tex.pqcrypto.slh_dsa
    Full FIPS 205 binding via liboqs 0.15. SLH-DSA-128s/128f/192s/256s
    with built-in sign-then-verify fault countermeasure per ePrint
    2026/759 (NXP, Apr 17 2026). SlhDsaFaultDetected exception carries
    structured metadata for telemetry. SLH-DSA-256s is the CNSA 2.0
    mandated path for software/firmware signing.

* tex.pqcrypto.threshold_ml_dsa
    k-of-n quorum signing across THRESHOLD_ML_DSA_44/65/87.
    QuorumDescriptor with SHA-256 commitment binding the policy.
    Sybil resistance via duplicate-index rejection. Forward-compatible
    with the Mithril MPC scheme (ePrint 2026/013) once the Rust crate
    gets a Python binding (placeholder gated behind MITHRIL_BACKEND).

* tex.pqcrypto.composite_ml_dsa
    Composite signatures per draft-ietf-lamps-pq-composite-sigs-18
    (Apr 9 2026). COMPOSITE_ML_DSA_65_ED25519 (~3,377 byte signatures)
    and COMPOSITE_ML_DSA_87_ECDSA_P384 (~4,733 byte signatures).
    HPKE-style domain separators from draft revision 16, non-
    separability enforced. For BSI 2021 / ANSSI 2024 jurisdictions.

* tex.pqcrypto.evidence_quorum
    Production glue between threshold provider and evidence chain.
    EvidenceQuorumPolicy.from_env() reads TEX_EVIDENCE_QUORUM_K and
    TEX_EVIDENCE_QUORUM_N; quorum signing always uses
    THRESHOLD_ML_DSA_87 (CNSA 2.0 Level 5). Serialize/deserialize of
    the embedded pq_quorum_signature payload with descriptor
    self-consistency check.

What this lets Tex tell customers
---------------------------------
- U.S. NSS / defence supply chain (CNSA 2.0 Jan 2027 procurement gate):
  ML-KEM-1024 + ML-DSA-87 + SLH-DSA-256s present and tested. No other
  AI governance product ships this stack.
- EU / DACH (BSI, ANSSI): composite ML-DSA per the current IETF LAMPS
  draft. Microsoft AGT and Asqav ship ML-DSA-65 alongside Ed25519 as
  independent credentials, not as a composite signature — a single
  break voids the entire scheme. Tex's composite requires both halves.
- Long-lived audit trails (EU AI Act, 10+ year retention): threshold
  ML-DSA-87 quorum signing means a single-key compromise — by an
  insider, a stolen HSM, or a future quantum break against an HSM
  that didn't migrate — cannot forge an evidence record. k of n must
  collude or be compromised.
